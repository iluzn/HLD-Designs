<h1>Minimum Cost to Hire K Workers</h1>
<span class="lc-diff hard">Hard</span>
<details class="lc-topics"><summary>Topics</summary><div class="lc-tags"><span class="lc-tag">Array</span><span class="lc-tag">Greedy</span><span class="lc-tag">Sorting</span><span class="lc-tag">Heap</span></div></details>
<p>There are <code>n</code> workers. Worker <code>i</code> has quality <code>nums[i]</code> (first n/2 of the array) and minimum wage expectation <code>nums[i]</code> (second n/2). You want to hire exactly <code>target</code> workers. In a paid group, each worker is paid in proportion to their quality relative to the group, and each must receive at least their minimum wage. Return the minimum total cost (integer part) to hire exactly <code>target</code> workers.</p><p><b>Encoding:</b> <code>nums</code> has 2k elements: first k are qualities, next k are wages. <code>target</code> is the number to hire.</p>
<h2>Example 1</h2><pre>Input:  nums = [10,20,5,70,50,30], target = 2
Output: 105</pre>
<h2>Example 2</h2><pre>Input:  nums = [3,1,10,10,1,1], target = 2
Output: 2</pre>
<h2>Constraints</h2><ul><li>2 &lt;= n &lt;= 50 (n = nums.length / 2)</li><li>1 &lt;= target &lt;= n</li><li>1 &lt;= quality[i], wage[i] &lt;= 10^4</li></ul>
