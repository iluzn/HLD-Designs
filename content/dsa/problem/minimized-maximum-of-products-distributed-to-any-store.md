<h1>Minimized Maximum of Products Distributed to Any Store</h1>
<span class="lc-diff medium">Medium</span>
<details class="lc-topics"><summary>Topics</summary><div class="lc-tags"><span class="lc-tag">Array</span><span class="lc-tag">Binary Search</span></div></details>
<p>You have <code>n</code> product types. The <code>i</code>th type has <code>nums[i]</code> units in stock. You want to distribute <strong>all</strong> units across <code>target</code> specialty stores with these rules:</p><ul><li>Each store carries <strong>at most one</strong> product type (but as many units of that type as you assign).</li><li>Some stores may remain empty.</li></ul><p>Minimize the <strong>maximum</strong> number of units assigned to any single store. Return that minimized maximum.</p>
<h2>Example 1</h2><pre>Input:  nums = [11,6], target = 10
Output: 2
Explanation: Assign product 0 (11 units) across 6 stores: ceil(11/2)=6 stores each getting at most 2. Assign product 1 (6 units) across 3 stores: ceil(6/2)=3 stores each getting at most 2. Uses 9 stores total (<=10). The max any store gets is 2.</pre>
<h2>Example 2</h2><pre>Input:  nums = [15,10,10], target = 7
Output: 5
Explanation: With max=5: product 0 needs ceil(15/5)=3, product 1 needs ceil(10/5)=2, product 2 needs ceil(10/5)=2. Total=7 stores. With max=4: 4+3+3=10>7. So 5 is the answer.</pre>
<h2>Constraints</h2><ul><li>1 &lt;= nums.length &lt;= 10^5</li><li>1 &lt;= nums[i] &lt;= 10^5</li><li>nums.length &lt;= target &lt;= 10^5</li></ul>
